T90 Titans League returns bigger and better than ever! This recurring competition provides a consistent, high-level stage for established pros to sharpen their skills, while giving newcomers the chance to prove themselves and break into the top tier. Across the first four seasons, 24 players competed in both the Platinum and Gold Leagues, with Silver serving as a qualifier. For season 5, the format expands: Silver becomes a full league, and Bronze takes its place as the qualifier, opening the door for 24 more players to battle through a full group stage and climb the ladder. With a $100,000 prize pool spread across more than 100 competitors, T90 Titans League delivers one of the most open and rewarding pathways into the Age of Empires pro scene.

T90 Titans League Season 5 follows the rules of the Unified Tournament Handbook with the following changes:

  • All players must be in a dedicated Discord voice chat while playing their set, to allow admins to contact them if needed.
  • During the group stage, if players are more than 10 minutes late, they will not receive any prize money for games played in that set. However, there will not be an admin loss for game 1. Being more than 20 minutes late without prior communication still results in an admin loss for the full set (3-0).
  • In case of a new patch or DLC, players will be informed as soon as possible if the new content will be used, or if the tournament continues on an old patch.
  • Additional rule changes will be published here before the start of the event. All other rules default to the Unified Tournament Handbook.

All breaches (including those not detailed in this handbook) will be handled at the sole discretion of the Tournament admins, in accordance with the authority granted by the Unified Tournament Handbook.

2. TTL Season 5 Format

For the first time, the main season of TTL will feature 3 full leagues: Platinum, Gold and Silver. All leagues consist of 24 players split into 4 groups of 6. After a round robin of play-all-3 sets players can be promoted or relegated between different leagues, and the top players in the Platinum league will battle in the playoffs to become the champion of TTL season 5.

T90 Titans League 5 Format

At the end of the group stage, the players finishing in 5th or 6th in their groups are relegated to a lower league. The players that are relegated from Silver will be eligible to play in Bronze league in season 6 to regain their spot.

In the Platinum league, the top 3 per group advances to the knockout stage. In Gold and Silver league, the top player from each group is automatically promoted to the league above their current league. All 2nd place players will face a 3rd place from another group in a promotion playoff to fight for promotion.

2.1 Group Stage Tiebreakers

In the group stage players will be ranked based on the number of games (maps) they have won.

Ties during the group stage will be handled in the following manner:

Games won > Sets won > Head to head > Win percentage in games against tied opponents > Total score of defeated opponents > Number of game 1 wins > Original seed

2.2 Group Stage Draw

Before the group stage draw players are placed in different pots based on their performance in the previous season. For example in Platinum league, pot 1 is filled with all semi finalists from Platinum season 4, and pot 6 is filled with players that won the Gold promotion playoffs in season 4. All pots have 4 players each, which will be split over the 4 groups in their specific league.

8. Event Coverage - All Coverage

  1. All broadcasters (including players) must register here their intention to stream any games, so that they can be given access to the appropriate Discord channels.
  2. All broadcasting must include the tournament logo before, after and in-between tournament games. Assets can be found in the #caster-resources channel on Discord, or at the end of this document.
  3. The tournament logo must be visible at all times while in-game.
  4. All broadcasters must add a custom chat command mentioning the sponsor, & linking to the host's stream & the announcement post or Liquipedia.
    1. Example text: !TTL = T90 Titans League is hosted by T90Official (https://twitch.tv/T90Official) & sponsored by World’s Edge. You can read more about it here - https://liquipedia.net/ageofempires/T90_Titans_League/5/Platinum_League
  5. Content creators are free to upload videos to video sharing platforms at their convenience. Games will be either available live or released alongside the main stream where they are being played in advance.
    1. When uploading gameplay from this event to other video sharing platforms, it must be clear, by using the title or description, who the tournament’s host is and what event it comes from.
    2. There must always be a direct clickable link to the official YouTube channel of the host (https://www.youtube.com/c/T90Official) in the description.
  6. T90 Titans League Silver and Gold group stage rounds 1 through 4 will be open casting according to player scheduling. Silver and Gold league group stage round 5, the promotion play-ins, all Platinum group stage matches and the Platinum playoffs will be cast live or as recorded games at specified times.

8.1. Players

  1. Players will be given a large degree of flexibility over scheduling, and are allowed to stream their POV.
    1. In Platinum league, players must play in a scheduled time slot if they want to stream their POV.
    2. For Gold and Silver week 5 and the Platinum/Gold play-ins, players must play in a scheduled time slot if they want to stream their POV.
  2. Players that stream their POV must use a two minute stream delay. In rare instances, players may be requested by admins to use a longer spectator delay.
    1. The spectator delay MUST match the stream delay (Standard 2 minutes).
    2. Players must not have access to any information which could give an unfair advantage at any stage.
    3. Players streaming their games via Twitch/Facebook/YouTube/Douyu etc. need to disable text to speech alerts and ensure that they cannot see their chat during their matches. Reading or interacting with chat between the start of the draft and the end of the last game can lead to a prize money deduction or immediate disqualification.

8.2. Casters

  1. In the event that there are multiple matches being played at the same time, best efforts will be made to provide casters with recorded games that are spoiler free.
